What it takes to be a freelancer

You need to have a hard skill.

You need to have a hard skill that businesses will pay for.

Or private clients will pay $$$$ for.

You need to be able to see a project from beginning to completion by yourself.

Assets you'll need

portfolio website

social media accounts

your identity = your business

(dont' make it sound like a side hustle)

How to find clients

Ideally they come to you

For me: SEO, referral, recurring

Possibly for y'all: Social Media, referral

How to get clients

Start with a free consultation meeting (phone or IRL)

Send proposal

What do I charge for a project?

Know that 30% is going bye bye for taxes

Know your life expenses (what is bare minimum, comfortable, successful)

Don't price like you'll be invoicing 40 hrs of work every week

Anatomy of a Proposal

Get a template together that you'll never have to spend more than 20 minutes

Goal for project

Services / Scope of work

Services NOT included

Extra costs NOT included

Pricing (flat w structure or hourly w estimate)

Yay client says YES!

Next: Contract

Scope of Work / Change Orders

Payment structure / rules

Limitation of Liability

Now the work begins

Time management

Proper work space

Good communication

Excellent at what you do

You finish the project

and you get PAID!!

Save 30% for taxes

Put as much as possible into savings

Goal should be enough savings for 6 months - 1 year of no work

You are responsible for retirement savings

more money tips

You are a business so you can make business deductions

Rent, Utlities

Office supplies (computer, desk, supplies)

Health insurance is 100% tax deductible
